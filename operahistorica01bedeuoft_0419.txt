There is a famous monastery in the province of
Lindsey named Beardaneu,2 which the same queen
and her husband Ethelred did greatly love, honour
and reverence, wherein she desired to lay up her
unele's honoured bones: and when the chariot was
come, wherein the same bones were brought at the
fall of the evening unto the foresaid monastery, the
men that were in the monastery would not gladly
admit them: because, although they knew him to be
a holy man, notwithstanding, forasmuch as he was
sprung from another province and took upon him to
be king over them, they pursued him also after his
death with the hatred they had of old. Whereby it
came to pass that that same night the relies that
